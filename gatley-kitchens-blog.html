Kitchens · Kitchen Islands

Island Life: The Latest Kitchen Island Trends

Kitchen islands have become one of the most requested features we design — and it's easy to see why. They add worktop space, storage, seating, and a natural focal point that pulls a kitchen together.

Multifunctional islands are the big shift — sinks, hobs, wine fridges and breakfast bars all built into one run of furniture. Alongside that, we're seeing larger islands, curved or angled shapes that soften the layout, hidden appliances for a cleaner look, and statement pendant lighting that turns the island into a proper centrepiece.

Worktop choice matters more on an island than anywhere else — it's the most visible surface in the room and takes the most punishment. Quartz is by far the most popular choice for that reason: hardwearing, easy to clean, and available in finishes that work with almost any door style.

If you're considering an island — or wondering whether your space could fit one — it's worth a conversation. More kitchens can accommodate one than people think.
