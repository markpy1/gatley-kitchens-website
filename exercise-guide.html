Push Day

Tuesday — Chest, Shoulders & Triceps

Bench · Dumbbells · Cables · ~45 mins
Tip: Rest 90 seconds between sets. Control the weight down for 2–3 seconds every rep — the slow lowering phase is where muscle is built. Don't rush it.
If the cage is free: swap the flat dumbbell press for barbell bench press and the shoulder press for barbell OHP. Both are better. But the dumbbell programme below is complete on its own.
HEAVIEST MOVEMENT FIRST · NEVER SACRIFICE FORM FOR WEIGHT
Flat Dumbbell Bench Press
Chest Mass
Bench + Dumbbells
4×8
sets × reps
Sit on bench, dumbbells on thighs, kick them up as you lie back. Retract your shoulder blades and keep them pinned throughout. Lower to mid-chest, elbows at 45° — not flared out wide. Press up and very slightly in. 2–3 seconds down every rep.
📈 Your primary chest builder. Week 1: find a weight you can do 8 clean reps. Add 2kg or 1 extra rep each week. Hit 4×10 before going up in weight.

Incline Dumbbell Press
Upper Chest
Adjustable Bench + Dumbbells
3×10
sets × reps
Bench at 30–45°. Don't go steeper — it becomes a shoulder exercise. Lower slowly, feel the stretch across the upper chest. Press up and slightly in. This builds the upper pec shelf that's visible in a t-shirt.
📈 Add 1–2kg each week. Hit 3×12 before moving up in weight.

Cable Chest Fly
Chest Isolation + Squeeze
Cable Machine
3×12
sets × reps
Cables at chest height. Stand central, slight forward lean. Arms slightly bent — keep that bend, elbows never lock. Bring hands together in an arc, squeeze the chest hard at centre for a full second. Return slowly — feel the stretch. Light weight, all feel.
📈 A finisher, not a heavy lift. The squeeze and stretch is the point. David Lloyd's cables are ideal for this.

Seated Dumbbell Shoulder Press
Shoulder Mass
Upright Bench + Dumbbells
3×10
sets × reps
Bench fully upright. Dumbbells at ear height, palms forward. Press straight up, bring them close but don't clang at the top. Lower to ear height — full range every rep. Seated removes momentum and forces the shoulders to do all the work.
📈 Don't rush to go heavy — shoulder injuries take months to heal. Build the habit of controlled reps first.

Lateral Raises
Side Delts — Width
Dumbbells
3×15
sets × reps
Light dumbbells, slight elbow bend. Lead with the elbows, not the hands. Raise to just below shoulder height — higher shifts it to traps. Slow and controlled both ways. These build the side delt width that makes you look broader.
📈 8kg done properly beats 15kg with momentum every time. Go lighter than you think.

Cable Tricep Pushdown
Triceps — Rope or Bar
Cable Machine
3×12
sets × reps
High cable, rope or bar. Elbows pinned to your sides — they never move. Push down until arms fully extend, squeeze the triceps hard. Return slowly for the stretch. Lean slightly forward for better range of motion.
📈 Triceps are ⅔ of arm size. This is the fastest route to noticeably bigger arms. Use the rope for better squeeze at the bottom.

Pull Day

Wednesday — Back & Biceps

Cables · Dumbbells · ~45 mins
Tip: Think about pulling with your elbows, not your hands — your hands are just hooks. Initiate every row by pulling the shoulder blades back first. That's what builds the back.
If the cage is free: start with barbell bent-over rows (4×8) before cables and dumbbells. Serious back thickness. But the cable + dumbbell programme below is complete on its own.
BACK IS YOUR BIGGEST MUSCLE — BUILD IT AND EVERYTHING LOOKS BIGGER
Seated Cable Row
Back Thickness
Cable Machine
4×10
sets × reps
Sit tall, slight forward lean to start. Pull elbows back past your body, squeezing shoulder blades hard at the end. Don't lean back to help — that's your lower back doing the work, not your back muscles. Return slowly and let shoulder blades fully stretch forward. That stretch matters.
📈 Primary back builder for this day. If you can't feel it in your back, drop the weight and focus on the squeeze.

Lat Pulldown
Back Width — V-Shape
Cable Machine
4×10
sets × reps
Wide overhand grip. Lean back slightly. Depress your shoulder blades before you pull — this is the key most people miss. Drive elbows down and in toward your hips. Pull to upper chest. Return slowly over 3 seconds — a slow return builds width fast. Don't let shoulders shrug up at the top.
📈 Builds lat width — the muscle that creates the V-shape. When you can do 4×12 comfortably, add weight.

Single-Arm Dumbbell Row
Back Thickness — Unilateral
Bench + Dumbbell
3×10
each side
Knee and hand on bench. Dumbbell hangs straight down — let it fully stretch the lat. Row elbow up and back past your hip. Don't rotate your torso. Go heavy — your back can handle more than you think. Full stretch at the bottom every single rep.
📈 One of the best back exercises. Don't shortchange the range of motion — the stretch at the bottom is where the growth happens.

Face Pulls
Rear Delts + Rotator Cuff
Cable — Rope Attachment
3×15
sets × reps
Cable at head height, rope attachment. Pull toward your face, elbows high and flared out. End position: hands beside your ears, elbows pointing sideways. Squeeze and hold a second. Return slowly. Light weight — this is shoulder health and posture work.
📈 Most people neglect rear delts and rotator cuff. These protect your shoulders from injury and fix rounded posture. Do them every pull session without fail.

Biceps Finisher
Incline Dumbbell Curl
Bicep Peak
Incline Bench + Dumbbells
3×10
sets × reps
Bench at 45–60°, lean back, arms hang straight down. This stretches the bicep at the bottom — a stretched muscle under load grows faster. Curl up, squeeze hard, lower slowly over 3 seconds. Don't swing the arms forward. This variation is one of the best for bicep peak specifically.
📈 The hanging stretch position is what makes this special — keep arms hanging back throughout the whole movement.

Cable Curl
Bicep — Constant Tension
Cable Machine
3×12
sets × reps
Low pulley, straight or EZ bar. Elbows pinned to sides. Cable keeps tension on the bicep throughout the full range — including at the top where dumbbells go slack. Curl up, squeeze, lower for 3 seconds. No swinging.
📈 The constant tension is why cables build a different kind of pump than dumbbells. Use both for complete development.

Legs + Core

Thursday — Legs, Glutes & Abs

Machines · Dumbbells · Cables · ~45 mins
Tip: Leg training triggers more testosterone and growth hormone than any other session. Don't skip this — it builds the whole body, not just legs. The hardest day is the most important one.
If the cage is free: replace the goblet squat with barbell back squats (4×8) and do them first. Nothing compares for full body growth. The programme below is complete without it though.
SQUAT DEEP · HINGE HARD · GROW EVERYWHERE
Leg Press
Quad + Glute Mass
Leg Press Machine
4×10
sets × reps
Feet shoulder-width, mid-platform. Lower slowly until knees are at 90° — don't let your lower back peel off the seat. Press through the whole foot. Don't lock out the knees at the top — keep tension on the muscle throughout. David Lloyd will have quality kit for this.
📈 Primary leg builder without needing the rack. Go heavy and progress every single week. This is where loading up makes the biggest visible difference quickly.

Goblet Squat
Quads + Hip Depth
Heavy Dumbbell
3×12
sets × reps
Heavy dumbbell held vertically at chest. Feet shoulder-width, toes out. Drop deep — elbows push knees out at the bottom. Pause at the bottom for a second. Drive up through the whole foot. The front-loaded weight forces better posture and deeper range than most squat variations.
📈 Go as heavy as you can while staying deep. When you can do 3×15, grab a heavier dumbbell.

Romanian Deadlift
Hamstrings + Glutes
Heavy Dumbbells
3×10
sets × reps
Stand tall with heavy dumbbells in front, soft knees. Hinge at the hips — push hips back, not knees down. Dumbbells stay close to your legs. Feel the deep stretch in the hamstrings at the bottom. Drive hips forward to come up — squeeze glutes hard at the top. Back stays flat throughout.
📈 The best hamstring and glute builder. Also fixes lower back stiffness when done correctly. The hamstrings need serious load to grow — go heavier than feels comfortable.

Dumbbell Walking Lunges
Quads + Glutes + Balance
Dumbbells
3×10
each leg
Dumbbells at sides. Step forward, drop the back knee toward (not to) the floor. Front knee over front foot — don't let it cave inward. Drive through the front heel to step through. Torso upright throughout. Use the gym floor space — walk the length and back.
📈 Excellent for quad and glute development and fixing left/right imbalances. One of the best functional leg exercises there is.

Core Finisher
Cable Crunch
Abs — Weighted
Cable Machine — Rope
3×15
sets × reps
Kneel facing cable, rope at head. Crunch elbows to knees by contracting your abs — don't pull with arms or hips. The movement comes from curling the ribcage toward the pelvis. Squeeze at the bottom, return to full stretch at the top. Weighted abs grow like any other muscle.
📈 Far more effective than endless floor crunches. Weighted ab work builds actual visible muscle.

Plank
Core Stability
Bodyweight
3×45s
hold
Forearms and toes. Squeeze everything simultaneously — glutes, abs, quads. Straight line head to heels, no sagging hips. Breathe steadily. Add a weight plate on your back when this becomes easy. A strong core makes every other lift heavier.
📈 Progress: 3×45s → 3×60s → 3×60s with 10kg plate. Core strength directly transfers to your bench, rows and everything else.

Supplement Stack

Your 12-Week Protocol

Nutrition + supplementation guide for maximum results
Bottom line: Supplements accelerate results — they don't replace training and protein. Get both right first, then the stack amplifies everything.
Daily Non-Negotiables
Creatine Monohydrate — 5g daily. The most researched supplement that works. Take it every day — timing doesn't matter. Builds strength and muscle fullness. ~£15 for 3 months. Start this today.
Gold Standard Whey — 2 scoops per day. Hit 180–200g total protein daily from all sources. Eggs, chicken, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese for the rest. Whey around training is convenient but not essential.
Vitamin D3 + K2 — 4,000IU daily. Most people in the UK are deficient year-round. Supports testosterone, immunity and recovery.
ZMA (Zinc + Magnesium) — before bed. Testosterone support and improved sleep quality. Works well alongside MK-677.
MK-677 Protocol (25mg / night)
Take 25mg 30 minutes before bed. GH pulses naturally during sleep — this amplifies the peak. Taking it at night minimises daytime lethargy.
Hunger will spike significantly. Channel this into hitting protein and calorie targets — not junk food. The hunger is a tool, not a free pass.
Weeks 1–2: Expect lethargy and water retention — this settles. Muscle fullness and strength increases kick in around week 3–4.
Sleep quality: Many users report significantly deeper sleep. Track on WHOOP — look for HRV improvements from week 3 onwards.
Source check: Only buy from suppliers with third-party COA (certificate of analysis). Underdosed products are widespread. Don't cut corners.
Calorie & Protein Targets
Protein: 180–200g daily. The single most important variable. Nothing else matters if this isn't hit consistently.
Calorie surplus: +300–500 above maintenance. Rough estimate: bodyweight in lbs × 15 = maintenance. Add 400 on top. Don't use the MK hunger as a green light to wildly overeat.
Realistic 12-week expectation: 3–5kg of solid size with this stack, training 3× weekly, protein on point. Some is water retention from creatine and MK — the muscle underneath is real and permanent.
