Quartz Worktop Care Guide

Quartz is a durable and low-maintenance material that will last for years with proper care. Follow these simple guidelines to keep your worktop looking pristine.

Daily Cleaning

✓ DO

  • Clean with warm water and mild dish soap
  • Use a soft microfibre cloth or non-abrasive sponge
  • Wipe spills immediately, especially acidic substances
  • Rinse thoroughly with clean water
  • Dry with a soft cloth to prevent water spots
  • Use cutting boards when preparing food
  • Use trivets or heat pads under hot pots and pans

✗ DON'T

  • Use bleach, ammonia, or harsh chemicals
  • Use abrasive cleaners or scouring pads
  • Use wax or oil-based polishes
  • Place hot pans directly on the surface
  • Cut directly on the worktop
  • Leave spills sitting on the surface
  • Use strong chemical products like oven cleaner

Additional Care Tips

Heat Protection

Although quartz is heat-resistant, extreme temperatures can cause damage. Always use trivets or heat pads for hot cookware.

Stain Prevention

Quartz is highly stain-resistant, but acidic foods, wine, coffee, and strongly pigmented substances should be wiped up promptly.

Scratch Prevention

While quartz is scratch-resistant, always use cutting boards. Avoid dragging heavy or rough objects across the surface.

Impact Protection

Avoid dropping heavy objects or placing excessive weight on unsupported overhangs to prevent chips or cracks.

With the correct care, your quartz worktop will maintain its beauty and durability for many years to come.
