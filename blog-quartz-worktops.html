Quartz Worktops Explained

What makes quartz different from granite or solid surface, how durable it really is, and what to look out for when choosing.

Quartz worktops have become increasingly popular in modern kitchens in recent years. This engineered stone is a great alternative to natural stone worktops, as it is just as durable and resistant to scratches, stains, and heat. In this guide, we'll explore the benefits of quartz worktops and discuss why they are a great choice for any kitchen.

What are Quartz Worktops?

Quartz worktops are made from a combination of natural quartz stone and a binding resin. The mixture is then moulded and shaped to create a durable, non-porous worktop that is resistant to scratches, stains, and heat. Quartz worktops are available in a wide range of colours and designs, making them a versatile choice for any kitchen.

Benefits of Quartz Worktops

There are several benefits to choosing quartz worktops for your kitchen.

Durable and long-lasting — Quartz worktops are incredibly durable and can last for many years. They are resistant to scratches, stains, and heat, making them a practical choice for a busy kitchen.

Easy to clean and maintain — The non-porous surface of quartz worktops makes them easy to clean and maintain. They do not require any special cleaning products or treatments, and spills and stains can be wiped away with ease.

Hygienic — The non-porous surface also means they do not harbour bacteria or germs, making them a safe and healthy choice for food preparation.

Versatile — Quartz worktops are available in a range of colours and designs. They can be used to create a modern, minimalist look or a traditional, classic style.

Heat-resistant — They can withstand high temperatures without becoming damaged, making them a practical choice for use with hot pans and dishes.

Low maintenance — Quartz worktops do not require any sealing or special treatments, making them an easy choice for busy households.

Design Options

Quartz worktops are available in a wide range of colours and designs, making them a versatile choice for any kitchen. They can be used to create a modern, minimalist look or a traditional, classic style. The material can also be moulded and shaped to create custom designs and features, such as integrated sinks or draining grooves.

We supply a range of quartz worktops from local suppliers to the market leaders such as Caesarstone and Silestone — which you can view in our Gatley showroom.

Installation

Quartz worktops should be installed by a professional to ensure a high-quality finish. The worktops are usually templated and cut to size off-site before being installed in the kitchen. The worktops can be joined seamlessly, creating a smooth and continuous surface.

In conclusion, quartz kitchen worktops are a durable, hygienic, and versatile choice for modern kitchens. They offer a range of benefits, including easy maintenance, heat resistance, and a wide range of design options. When choosing a worktop, it's important to consider the practical aspects of the material, as well as the overall style and design of the kitchen. Quartz worktops offer a practical and stylish solution for any kitchen.
